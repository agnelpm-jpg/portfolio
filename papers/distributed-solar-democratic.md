## Distributed Solar Infrastructure and Democratic Governance

### A compact Mumbai energy framework

Author: Agnel Suresh  
Status: Short-form research note

## Executive Summary

Mumbai can meet its urban energy needs through distributed rooftop solar, MVDC transmission, and a commodity-backed settlement layer governed by a democratic coordination council.

## Core Proposal

1. Technical: rooftop solar + MVDC + batteries
2. Settlement: MET as energy-backed commodity exposure
3. Governance: 21-member committee with civil society seats

## Technical Architecture

- Rooftop solar potential: 1.72 GW
- MVDC network: 200 km, 8 corridors, ±10 kV DC
- Storage: 500–1,000 MWh for peak shaving and settlement stability

Key principle: local energy flows reduce losses and enable resilient urban dispatch.

## Settlement Framework

- 1 MET = 1 kWh equivalent exposure
- Contracts settle through MCX-style commodity mechanisms
- CBDC integration is optional, not required

Price corridor:
- Floor: ₹2.86/MET
- Ceiling: ₹7.50/MET
- Operational band: ₹3.36–₹6.50/MET

## Governance Model

Committee composition:
- Municipal reps: 5
- Civil society: 6
- Technical experts: 4
- Industry reps: 4
- Consumer reps: 2

Accountability:
- Annual audit
- Quarterly technical review
- Public dashboards
- Grievance and transparency process

## Risk and Mitigation

- Regulatory delay: multiple approval pathways
- Utility resistance: municipal and consumer ownership
- Market volatility: energy backing + price corridor
- Governance capture: civil society oversight and audits

## Conclusion

A concise, pilot-ready framework for Mumbai: resilient distributed energy, commodity-settlement discipline, and democratic infrastructure governance.
