# UQCIT + NQG-STF

## Abstract

UQCIT/NQG-STF proposes a deterministic cosmology in which gravity remains non-quantized, quantum behavior emerges from perturbations in a continuous spacetime fabric, dark-sector effects arise from residual field binding, and singularities are replaced by sealed meta-singularity dynamics.

## Core Thesis

The framework keeps General Relativity at the geometric level while treating quantum probability as an emergent coarse-grained effect:

\[
\text{GR Geometry} + \text{STF Perturbations} \Rightarrow \text{Emergent Quantum Behavior}
\]

The model replaces a fundamental cosmological constant with a dynamic tension term:

\[
\Lambda \rightarrow \Lambda_{\mathrm{dyn}}(g,\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{STF}},\mathrm{RFBE})
\]

## Time Decomposition

Cosmic time is split into three modes:

\[
\tau = \{\tau_v,\tau_c,\tau_p\}
\]

- \(\tau_v\): local relativistic/proper time.
- \(\tau_c\): causal-entropic cosmological arrow.
- \(\tau_p\): perpetual cycle-ordering time that persists across cosmic cycles.

This lets \(\tau_c\) reset at cycle onset while \(\tau_p\) preserves ordering across cycles.

## Singularity Replacement

Instead of infinite-density singularities, collapse forms a sealed meta-singularity (MS). Internal instability grows through QIB/IS structures until the quantum singularity structural limit (QSSL) is reached, after which spacetime fabric tears into a universal expansion outburst.
