# MET: Commodity-Linked Settlement For Distributed Urban Solar

## Abstract

MET proposes a settlement layer for Mumbai rooftop solar. It is not a cryptocurrency and not a consumer payment token. It is a commodity-linked accounting unit backed by verified electricity generation and settled through regulated escrow/market infrastructure.

## Core Definition

\[
1\ \mathrm{MET} = 1\ \mathrm{kWh}
\]

One MET represents one verified kilowatt-hour delivered into the settlement system. The point is to make distributed generation financeable, measurable, and hedgeable without inventing a new currency.

## Problem

Mumbai has significant rooftop solar potential, but deployment is slowed by fragmented incentives, delayed EPC payments, weak prosumer participation, and lack of market-linked settlement. The technical issue is solvable; the coordination layer is missing.

## Mechanism

The proposed structure is:

\[
\text{Verified Solar Output} \rightarrow \text{MET Units} \rightarrow \text{Escrow Settlement} \rightarrow \text{Commodity-Linked Exposure}
\]

Tripartite escrow coordinates EPCs, buyers, and settlement agents. Commodity-market exposure provides price discovery and hedging while keeping the instrument inside existing regulatory corridors.

## Regulatory Position

MET is designed to align with SEBI, MCX, RBI, and MERC logic. It avoids claims of ownership over physical electrons and avoids retail payment-token behavior.

## Essence

MET is a financial coordination primitive for distributed solar. It connects rooftop generation to settlement certainty, market pricing, and faster deployment.
